Pakistan - Land of the Pure

The genesis of the lake lies in a landslide in the Attabad village within the Hunza Valley, in Gilgit-Baltistan. The mesmerizing blue color of Attabad Lake results from the glacial minerals in the surrounding mountains. The lake has become one of the biggest tourist attractions in Gilgit−Baltistan, offering activities like boating, jet-skiing, fishing, and other recreational activities.

Shangrila, is situated in the following Northern Areas of Pakistan. Deosai Plains; the longest, widest and highest plateau in the world. The fast flowing River Indus, which starts from Ladakh and cascades its way through the Karakoram and Himalayan ranges, along picturesque valleys, glaciers, natural wild life, forests and historical monuments, dating back thousands of years and across the country into the Arabian sea.

Towering at 28,251 feet, K2 is the second highest mountain in the world. It is located in the north-western Karakoram Mountain Range on the Pakistan—China border between Baltistan in Gilgit-Baltistan and the Taxkorgan Tajik Autonomous County of Xinjiang, China. K2 is known by many names: the Savage Mountain, Mount Godwin-Austen, Chhogori, Qogir. Extreme weather conditions and avalanches make a trek to K2 impossible during the winter. The trek to K2 is a strenuous, adrenaline-filled journey of around 3 weeks

Tucked away in the lap of the majestic Nanga Parbat, the ninth highest mountain in the world, Fairy Meadows is a spellbinding alpine meadow in the heart of the Gilgit-Baltistan region of Pakistan. Situated at an altitude of around 3,300 meters (10,827 feet) above sea level, this ethereal landscape enchants visitors with its lush greenery, vibrant wildflowers, and uninterrupted views of the towering peaks that surround it. Renowned for its surreal beauty and serene ambiance, Fairy Meadows beckons adventurers, nature enthusiasts, and photographers to immerse themselves in its pristine wilderness and bask in the tranquility of the Himalayan foothills.
